\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Logical reasoning with symbolic structure.}
ProofWriter \citep{tafjord2020proofwriter} evaluates natural-language rule
reasoning with facts, rules, labels, and proof traces. ProverGen/ProverQA
\citep{qi2025provergen} extends prover-backed logical-reasoning evaluation.
Logic-LM \citep{pan2023logiclm} couples language models to symbolic solvers,
while SymbCoT \citep{xu2024symbcot} and VeriCoT \citep{feng2025vericot} use
symbolic chains and consistency checks. Our work keeps test-time inference
model-generated and asks whether supervision can make the emitted proof,
refutation, and abstention evidence easier to verify.
Recent systems make the inference-time alternative sharper. VERUS-LM
\citep{callewaert2025veruslm} separates domain knowledge from queries and uses
symbolic solvers for richer logical tasks. NARS-Reasoning-v0.1
\citep{gabriel2026nars} translates natural-language problems into executable
Narsese and evaluates \texttt{True}/\texttt{False}/\texttt{Uncertain} through a
NARS engine. CGD-PD \citep{huang2026cgdpd} improves three-way logical QA by
probing both a hypothesis and its negation and using proof-driven
disambiguation at test time. These systems are stronger inference-time
pipelines than our supervised decoder; the comparison clarifies that \model{}
isolates a different axis, evidence-bearing supervision under a fixed
generation loop.

\paragraph{Abstention, abduction, and explanations.}
Abstention work studies when models should decline to answer
\citep{madhusudhan2025abstention}; our setting asks for a checkable reason for
declining. Missing-support witnesses are also related to abductive reasoning and
explanation generation \citep{bhagavatula2020abductive,camburu2018esnli}.
Unlike free-form explanations, our witnesses refer to absent literals or blocked
rules in an indexed theory. This connects to diagnosis and hitting-set views of
missing support \citep{reiter1987diagnosis}, although our verifier checks a
canonical blocked derivation rather than enumerating all minimal explanations.

\paragraph{Disproof and counterexamples.}
Recent formal disproof work generates machine-checked counterexamples for false
Lean statements \citep{li2026learningtodisprove}. Our unsupported queries are
different: neither the query nor its negation follows, so the desired evidence
is missing support rather than a counterexample.
