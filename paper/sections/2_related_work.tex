\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Logical reasoning with symbolic structure.}
ProofWriter \citep{tafjord2020proofwriter} evaluates natural-language rule
reasoning with facts, rules, labels, and proof traces. ProverGen/ProverQA
\citep{qi2025provergen} extends prover-backed logical-reasoning evaluation.
Logic-LM \citep{pan2023logiclm} couples language models to symbolic solvers,
while SymbCoT \citep{xu2024symbcot} and VeriCoT \citep{feng2025vericot} use
symbolic chains and consistency checks. Recent solver-coupled pipelines make
the inference-time alternative sharper: VERUS-LM \citep{callewaert2025veruslm}
and NARS-Reasoning-v0.1 \citep{gabriel2026nars} both move search, repair, or
disambiguation into test time. These methods are often practically stronger,
but they answer a different question because they change the decoding
procedure. ProCo keeps the decoding loop fixed and asks whether supervision
alone can make model-generated evidence easier to check.

The same distinction appears in test-time consistency and self-verification
wrappers. Logic-LM, SymbCoT, and VeriCoT all rely on external symbolic checks or
consistency filters to improve reliability. ProCo is complementary: it does not
replace those wrappers, but isolates the supervision-target axis under a fixed
model-generated output format.

\paragraph{Abstention, abduction, and explanations.}
Abstention work studies when models should decline to answer
\citep{madhusudhan2025abstention}; our setting asks for a checkable reason for
declining, not just a calibrated refusal. Missing-support witnesses are also
related to abductive reasoning and explanation generation
\citep{bhagavatula2020abductive,camburu2018esnli}. Unlike free-form
explanations, our witnesses refer to absent literals or blocked rules in an
indexed theory. This connects to classic diagnosis and hitting-set views of
missing support \citep{reiter1987diagnosis}, although our verifier checks a
canonical blocked derivation rather than enumerating all minimal explanations.

\paragraph{Disproof and counterexamples.}
Recent formal disproof work generates machine-checked counterexamples for false
Lean statements \citep{li2026learningtodisprove}. Our unsupported queries are
different: neither the query nor its negation follows, so the desired evidence
is missing support rather than a counterexample.
