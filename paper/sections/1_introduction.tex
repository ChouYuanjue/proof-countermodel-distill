\section{Introduction}

Logical reasoning systems are often evaluated by whether they answer correctly.
For open-world rule reasoning, that is incomplete. If a theory does not entail a
query or its negation, the correct label is \texttt{Unknown}; but a bare
\texttt{Unknown} does not say why the theory is insufficient or which support is
missing.

ProofWriter \citep{tafjord2020proofwriter} makes this question concrete. Each
example contains indexed facts and rules, and each query is \texttt{True},
\texttt{False}, or \texttt{Unknown} under open-world semantics. Proof and
refutation have natural evidence: derive the query, or derive the opposite
literal. The unsupported case needs a different kind of evidence: a witness
that some needed premise or rule application is absent from the closure.

We study whether this behavior can be learned through supervision alone, under a
fixed generation loop and without solver calls at inference. \model{} uses a
single structured output format with three modes:
\prove{} emits a derivation chain for true queries, \refute{} emits a derivation
of the opposite literal for false queries, and \abstain{} emits a failed-rule or
missing-support witness for unsupported queries. A symbolic checker then
replays proof and refutation chains and checks abstention witnesses against the
theory closure. The central metric is joint label-plus-verifier-accepted
evidence accuracy.

One might dismiss this as target-format engineering on a synthetic benchmark.
That framing misses the question we actually test. Ordinary proof supervision
under-specifies \texttt{Unknown}; evidence-bearing abstention turns the label
into a checkable object. The contribution is deliberately narrow: can
supervision alone make a model emit such evidence without test-time repair?
Because the same symbolic structure can also generate canonical witnesses, a
solver-assisted oracle or repair system is a strong alternative baseline; ProCo
instead isolates what the model learns to emit without post-hoc repair.

The main empirical result is simple. On a fixed three-seed 7B suite,
\model{} keeps raw accuracy competitive with proof-only supervision but more
than doubles joint accuracy: 39.9\% to 75.0\% in-domain and 26.5\% to 55.0\%
under depth shift. At the 4k budget, answer-only remains the stronger raw
classifier, so the value of \model{} is checkable evidence rather than raw
classification superiority. Proof-only models often learn when to say
\texttt{Unknown}, but their abstentions are not trained to carry missing-support
witnesses; \model{} turns many of those abstentions into checkable witnesses. A
support-deletion stress test, where facts or rules are removed from originally
provable questions until they become unsupported, shows the same behavior under
a controlled intervention.

Our contributions are:
\begin{enumerate}
\item We define a missing-support witness schema for unsupported ProofWriter-style indexed queries.
\item We implement a lightweight verifier for proof chains, refutation chains, and abstention evidence.
\item We introduce a support-deletion stress test that creates controlled unsupported cases from originally provable questions.
\item We provide seeded, scaling, transfer, and ablation evidence showing that missing-support supervision improves verifier-accepted abstention evidence under this scope.
\end{enumerate}
