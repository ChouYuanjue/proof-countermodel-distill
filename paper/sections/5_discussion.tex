\section{Discussion and Conclusion}
\label{sec:discussion}

\model{} improves the part of open-world reasoning that ordinary proof
supervision leaves underspecified: explaining why an unsupported query cannot be
derived. Proof-only models often learn the \texttt{Unknown} label, but their
reasons are generic. Missing-support supervision gives the model a concrete
object to emit and gives the verifier a concrete object to check.

The result should be read with its scope in mind. The experiments use
ProofWriter-style theories with indexed facts and rules, so the verifier can
align generated evidence to symbolic objects. Extending the method to noisier
free text would require a parser or alignment layer for entities, literals, and
rule paraphrases. The abstention verifier is also not a proof of unique minimal
causality: it checks a canonical blocked derivation, and the strict audit shows
that rule-blocked witnesses are less canonical than no-rule witnesses. A related
risk is evaluator coupling: the target constructor and verifier share the same
forward-chaining closure and blocked-derivation search. The support-deletion
stress test gives an intervention beyond naturally occurring unknown questions,
but an independently implemented checker would be a stronger guard against this
coupling. Finally, some robustness checks and ablations are single-seed; they
localize behavior but do not fully characterize variance.

The most useful next experiments are direct comparisons with solver-coupled
decoding or post-hoc repair on the same fixed subset, equivalence-aware
verification for alternative blocked derivations, multi-seed backbone checks,
harder support deletions involving multiple premises, and human audits of
whether faithful unknown witnesses are useful to readers.

We presented \model, a supervision format for proof, refutation, and
missing-support abstention. On ProofWriter-style rule reasoning, it turns many
otherwise generic \texttt{Unknown} answers into verifier-checkable evidence,
substantially improving joint label-plus-evidence accuracy while preserving
competitive raw accuracy.
